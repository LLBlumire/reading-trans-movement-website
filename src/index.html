Reading Trans Movement

The Reading Trans Movement is an activist group operating in the Reading area.

History

Founding Protest Against Holly Lawford-Smith and Rosa Freedman

The group was founded on the 23rd of April 2022 by Milan, to organise a protest against a talk being given by notorious transphobe Holly Lawford-Smith, and hosted by Rosa Freedman, who had previously doxxed a student criticising her transphobia in the Daily mail. The talk, entitled “Who Put the ‘GI’ in SOGI”, argued that it was incorrect for gender identity and sexual orientation to be considered together when nations implement bans on harmful conversion therapy practice. Following from this separation, the talk advocated that only conversion therapy applied to sexual orientation should be banned, thus advocating it is correct for transgender people to be subjected to torture.

Organisation began two days before the event, and rapidly spread from the student body into alumni and other local members of the trans, gender non-conforming, and allied community. Two days later, as the talk was going on, over 100 people gathered with signs and flags, protesting the talk. It is our belief that a total of 9 people attended the talks, two of which were students explicitly sent into the talk by Reading Trans Movement to monitor the talk for any content which would break the university of readings guidelines against hate speech. Two other men in the audience later identified themselves to Reading Trans Movement as students only in attendance to build a full understanding of what was said in the talk to better raise their own complaints about it with the university. Bringing the total number in attendance of the talk to 5, all of whom appear to be personal friends of Rosa Freedman and Holly Lawford-Smith, and went out with the two after the talk for a social gathering.

Open Letter To The University Of Reading

In parallel with the protest, an open letter was drafted and collaborated on by many members of the movement, and eventually was published online in several formats. The letter called for the University of Reading to:

  • Assess the numerous breaches of the universities policies which occured both in the content and presentation of the talk.
  • Perform due dilegence when inviting future guest speakers to the university, taking into account previous controversy surrounding speakers and hosts.
  • Publicly reaffirm its commitment to the rights of all individuals, including transgender and gender non-conforming people, to experience their university life in a safe manner, free from discrimination and harrassment.

The university of reading failed to adequately responded to the issues raised in the open letter regarding safe accessibility to the lecture, the history of Holly Lawford-Smith and Rosa Freedman in presenting harmful and hateful rhetoric. In short the university investigated itself, and found no wrongdoing. In companion to this, this vice chancellor released a statement which also addressed none of the concerns raised.

Vigil for Brianna Ghey

In the wake of the horrific murder of Brianna Ghey, a vigil was organised. The vigil took place in Forbury gardens, and provided a space for grief and community to the trans and gender non-conforming community in Reading. Reading pride has recorded and uploaded footage of the speeches, poetry, and songs presented at the vigil.
